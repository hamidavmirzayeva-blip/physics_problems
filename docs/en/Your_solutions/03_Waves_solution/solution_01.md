# Wave Properties Notes

We are given:  
- Frequency of sound wave: $f = 440 \ \text{Hz}$  
- Speed of sound in air: $v_\text{air} = 343 \ \text{m/s}$  
- Speed of sound in water: $v_\text{water} = 1482 \ \text{m/s}$  

---

## Formula for wavelength

The wavelength $\lambda$ is given by:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

where:  
- $\lambda$ = wavelength  
- $v$ = speed of sound in the medium  
- $f$ = frequency of the wave

---

## Wavelength in air

$$
\lambda_\text{air} = \frac{v_\text{air}}{f} = \frac{343}{440} \approx 0.78 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Wavelength in water

$$
\lambda_\text{water} = \frac{v_\text{water}}{f} = \frac{1482}{440} \approx 3.37 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Notes

- The frequency of a wave **does not change** when it moves from one medium to another.  
- The wavelength **changes** depending on the speed of sound in the medium.
