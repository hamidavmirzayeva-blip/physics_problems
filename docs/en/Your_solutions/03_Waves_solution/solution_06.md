# Wave Equation Analysis

We are given a wave described by:

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2 \pi x - 50 \pi t)
$$

where $y$ and $x$ are in meters, and $t$ is in seconds.  

We want to determine the **amplitude**, **wavelength**, **frequency**, and **wave speed**.

---

## Step 1: General Wave Form

A standard sinusoidal wave traveling in the +x direction:

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

Where:  
- $A$ = amplitude  
- $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$ = wave number  
- $\omega = 2\pi f$ = angular frequency  
- $f$ = frequency  
- $\lambda$ = wavelength  
- $v$ = wave speed  

---

## Step 2: Compare with Given Equation

From  

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2 \pi x - 50 \pi t)
$$

We identify:

- **Amplitude:** $A = 0.05\ \text{m}$  
- **Wave number:** $k = 2\pi \implies \lambda = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi} = 1\ \text{m}$  
- **Angular frequency:** $\omega = 50\pi \implies f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi} = 25\ \text{Hz}$  

---

## Step 3: Wave Speed

Wave speed is given by:

$$
v = f \cdot \lambda = 25 \cdot 1 = 25\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## ✅ Step 4: Summary Table

| Quantity        | Value        |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Amplitude $A$   | 0.05 m      |
| Wavelength $\lambda$ | 1 m     |
| Frequency $f$   | 25 Hz       |
| Wave speed $v$  | 25 m/s      |

> **Note:** Always compare the given wave equation to the standard form $y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$ to extract the wave properties directly.
