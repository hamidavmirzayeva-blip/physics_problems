# Standing Wave on a String

We are asked to find the **wavelength** of a standing wave on a string.

---

## Step 1: Formula for a standing wave

For a string **fixed at both ends**, the relationship between string length $L$ and wavelength $\lambda$ is:

$$
\lambda = \frac{2 L}{n}
$$

Where:  
- $L$ = length of the string  
- $n$ = number of **antinodes** (peaks) in the standing wave  
- $\lambda$ = wavelength of the wave  

> **Note:** Each harmonic has a number of antinodes equal to the harmonic number.

---

## Step 2: Given Values

- $L = 80\ \text{cm} = 0.8\ \text{m}$  
- Number of antinodes $n = 4$  

---

## Step 3: Calculation

$$
\lambda = \frac{2 L}{n} = \frac{2 \cdot 0.8}{4} = 0.4\ \text{m}
$$

---

## ✅ Answer

The wavelength of the standing wave is:

$$
\lambda = 0.4\ \text{m}
$$
