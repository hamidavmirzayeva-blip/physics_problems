# Phase Difference Between Two Points on a Wave

We want to calculate the **phase difference** between two points separated by a distance $\Delta x$ on a wave with wavelength $\lambda$.

---

## Formula

The phase difference $\Delta \phi$ is given by:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2 \pi \, \Delta x}{\lambda}
$$

where:  
- $\Delta \phi$ = phase difference in **radians**  
- $\Delta x$ = distance between the two points  
- $\lambda$ = wavelength of the wave  

---

## Given

$$
\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

---

## Calculation

Substitute $\Delta x$ into the formula:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2 \pi \left(\frac{\lambda}{3}\right)}{\lambda} = \frac{2 \pi}{3}\ \text{radians}
$$

---

## ✅ Answer

The phase difference between the two points is:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2 \pi}{3}\ \text{radians}
$$

> **Note:** The phase difference is proportional to the fraction of the wavelength separating the points. In this case, $\frac{1}{3}$ of a wavelength corresponds to $\frac{2 \pi}{3}$ radians.
