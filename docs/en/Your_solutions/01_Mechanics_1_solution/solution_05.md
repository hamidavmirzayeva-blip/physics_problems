

# 1. Understand the situation

* The **river flows east** at **2 m/s**
* The **boat speed in still water** = **5 m/s**
* The boat wants to go **straight north**

But the water pushes the boat **east**, so if the boat points straight north it will drift.

To go **directly north**, the boat must aim a little **west of north** to cancel the river current.

---

# 2. Break the boat velocity into components

Let the boat aim at an angle **θ west of north**.

Boat speed relative to water = **5 m/s**

So the components are:

North component = (5 \cos\theta)

West component = (5 \sin\theta)

---

# 3. Cancel the river current

The river pushes the boat **east at 2 m/s**.

To cancel that, the boat must have **2 m/s westward**.

So:

5 sinθ = 2

Now solve for θ.

sinθ = 2 / 5

sinθ = 0.4

θ = arcsin(0.4)

θ ≈ **23.6°**

So the boat must aim:

**23.6° west of north**

---

# 4. Find the northward speed

Now find how fast the boat moves **north**.

North component:

5 cosθ

cos(23.6°) ≈ 0.917

So:

North speed = 5 × 0.917

≈ **4.58 m/s**

---

# 5. Time to cross the river

River width = **200 m**

Time = distance / speed

time = 200 / 4.58

≈ **43.7 seconds**

---

# Final Answers

Direction to aim:
**23.6° west of north**

Time to cross river:
**about 43.7 seconds**

---

# Intuition

* The river pushes **east**
* The boat aims **slightly west**
* These cancel → the boat goes **straight north**
