# Tennis Ball Bouncing – Notes

## 🔹 Given

- Initial height: $h_0 = 2.0 \, \text{m}$  
- Energy loss per bounce: 30%  
- Gravity: $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$  

---

## 🧠 Step 1: Energy after first bounce

The ball **loses 30%** of its mechanical energy, so it **retains 70%**:

$$
E_1 = 0.7 \, E_0
$$

Since gravitational potential energy at height $h$:

$$
E = mgh
$$

Height after first bounce:

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \, h_0
$$

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \cdot 2.0
$$

$$
h_1 = 1.4 \, \text{m}
$$

---

## 🧠 Step 2: Height after second bounce

Energy after second bounce:

$$
E_2 = 0.7 \, E_1 = 0.7 \cdot 0.7 \, E_0 = 0.7^2 E_0
$$

Corresponding height:

$$
h_2 = 0.7^2 \, h_0
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.49 \cdot 2.0
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \, \text{m}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

- Height after **second bounce**:

$$
h_2 \approx 0.98 \, \text{m}
$$

---

## 📚 Key Concept

- **Mechanical energy after each bounce:** $E_n = (1 - f)^n E_0$, where $f$ = fraction of energy lost per bounce  
- Corresponding **height:** $h_n = (1 - f)^n h_0$
