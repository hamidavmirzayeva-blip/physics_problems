# Simple Pendulum – Notes

## 🌙 1️⃣ Period on the Moon

The formula for a simple pendulum:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

### Definitions
- $T$ — period (time of one oscillation)
- $L$ — length of the pendulum
- $g$ — gravitational acceleration

---

### Key Idea

For the same pendulum:
- Length $L$ is constant
- Only gravity $g$ changes

Thus:

$$
T \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{g}}
$$

---

### Moon vs Earth

On the Moon:

$$
g_{\text{moon}} = \frac{g_{\text{earth}}}{6}
$$

So:

$$
T_{\text{moon}} = T_{\text{earth}} \cdot \sqrt{\frac{g_{\text{earth}}}{g_{\text{moon}}}}
$$

Substitute:

$$
T_{\text{moon}} = 4 \cdot \sqrt{6}
$$

Approximation:

$$
\sqrt{6} \approx 2.45
$$

$$
T_{\text{moon}} \approx 4 \times 2.45 = 9.8 \text{ s}
$$

### ✅ Result
- Period on Moon: **$\approx 9.8$ s**

---

## 🌍 2️⃣ Length for a 1-Second Pendulum

Start from:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Solve for $L$:

$$
L = \frac{gT^2}{4\pi^2}
$$

---

### Substitute Values

- $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$
- $T = 1 \, \text{s}$

$$
L = \frac{9.8 \cdot 1^2}{4\pi^2}
$$

$$
4\pi^2 \approx 39.48
$$

$$
L = \frac{9.8}{39.48} \approx 0.248 \text{ m}
$$

---

### Convert Units

$$
0.248 \text{ m} \approx 25 \text{ cm}
$$

### ✅ Result
- Required length: **$\approx 0.25$ m (25 cm)**

---


  - Moon period: **$\approx 9.8$ s**
  - Length for $T=1$ s: **$\approx 0.25$ m**
