# Energy + Inelastic Collision – Notes

## 🔹 Given

- $m_1 = 0.5 \, \text{kg}$ (moving block)  
- $m_2 = 1.5 \, \text{kg}$ (at rest)  
- Height: $h = 3.0 \, \text{m}$  
- $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$  

---

## 🧠 Step 1: Speed Before Collision (Energy Conservation)

At the top:
- Potential Energy = $mgh$  
- Kinetic Energy = $0$

At the bottom:
- Potential Energy = $0$  
- Kinetic Energy = $\frac{1}{2}mv^2$

So:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Mass cancels:

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
$$

Solve for velocity:

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

---

### Substitute Values

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3.0}
$$

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{58.8}
$$

$$
v_1 \approx 7.67 \, \text{m/s}
$$

---

## 🧠 Step 2: Inelastic Collision (Momentum Conservation)

Since the blocks **stick together**, momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v
$$

Since $v_2 = 0$:

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v
$$

Solve for final velocity:

$$
v = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

---

### Substitute Values

$$
v = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$

$$
v = \frac{3.835}{2.0}
$$

$$
v \approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}
$$

---

## 📌 Final Answer

- Final speed after collision:

$$
v \approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}
$$
