# Mass-Spring Oscillation – Notes

## 🔹 Given

The motion is:

$$
x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)
$$

- Amplitude: $A = 0.2 \, \text{m}$
- Angular frequency: $\omega = 10\pi \, \text{rad/s}$
- Mass: $m = 10 \, \text{kg}$

---

## 🧠 Key Formulas

For a mass-spring system:

$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

$$
k = m\omega^2
$$

Total mechanical energy:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
$$

---

## 🔸 Step 1: Find Spring Constant $k$

$$
k = m\omega^2
$$

Substitute values:

$$
k = 10 \cdot (10\pi)^2
$$

$$
k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2
$$

$$
k = 1000\pi^2
$$

Approximate:

$$
\pi^2 \approx 9.87
$$

$$
k \approx 1000 \times 9.87 = 9870 \, \text{N/m}
$$

### ✅ Result:
- $k \approx 9870 \, \text{N/m}$

---

## 🔸 Step 2: Total Mechanical Energy

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
$$

Substitute:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1000\pi^2 \cdot (0.2)^2
$$

$$
(0.2)^2 = 0.04
$$

$$
E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1000\pi^2 \cdot 0.04
$$

$$
E = 500 \cdot 0.04 \cdot \pi^2
$$

$$
E = 20\pi^2
$$

Approximate:

$$
E \approx 20 \times 9.87 = 197.4 \, \text{J}
$$

### ✅ Result:
- $E \approx 197 \, \text{J}$

---


  $$
  E = 20\pi^2 \approx 197 \, \text{J}
  $$
