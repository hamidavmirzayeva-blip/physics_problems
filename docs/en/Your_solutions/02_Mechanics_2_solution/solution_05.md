# Runner + Cart Collision – Notes

## 🔹 Given

- Runner mass: $m_1 = 70 \, \text{kg}$  
- Runner speed: $v_1 = 3 \, \text{m/s}$  
- Cart mass: $m_2 = 140 \, \text{kg}$  
- Cart speed: $v_2 = 0$ (stationary)  

---

## 🧠 Step 1: Use Momentum Conservation

Since the runner **jumps onto the cart** and they move **together**, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v
$$

Substitute values:

$$
70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0 = (70 + 140)v
$$

$$
210 = 210 v
$$

Solve for $v$:

$$
v = \frac{210}{210} = 1 \, \text{m/s}
$$

### ✅ Result
- Final speed of cart + runner: **$v = 1 \, \text{m/s}$**

---

## 🧠 Step 2: Check Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy before collision:

$$
KE_{\text{initial}} = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^2
$$

$$
KE_{\text{initial}} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2 + 0
$$

$$
KE_{\text{initial}} = 35 \cdot 9 = 315 \, \text{J}
$$

Kinetic energy after collision:

$$
KE_{\text{final}} = \frac{1}{2} (m_1 + m_2)v^2
$$

$$
KE_{\text{final}} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2
$$

$$
KE_{\text{final}} = 105 \, \text{J}
$$

---

### ✅ Observation

- $KE_{\text{final}} < KE_{\text{initial}}$  
- **Kinetic energy is not conserved** because the collision is **perfectly inelastic**. Some energy is transformed into **heat, sound, or internal energy**.

---

## 📌 Final Answers

- Final speed: 

$$
v = 1 \, \text{m/s}
$$

- Kinetic energy: **not conserved** (inelastic collision)

---

## 📚 Key Concepts

1. **Momentum is always conserved** in collisions (no external force).  
2. **Kinetic energy is only conserved** in **elastic collisions**.  
3. Perfectly inelastic collision → objects stick together, kinetic energy decreases.
