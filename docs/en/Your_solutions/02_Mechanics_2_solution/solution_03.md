# Pendulum Speed at Lowest Point

## 🔹 Given

- Length: $L = 1.0 \, \text{m}$
- Initial angle: $\theta = 15^\circ$
- Gravitational acceleration: $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$

---

## 🧠 Key Idea (Energy Conservation)

At the top:
- Potential Energy (PE) = max
- Kinetic Energy (KE) = 0

At the bottom:
- PE = 0
- KE = max

So:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Mass cancels:

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
$$

---

## 🔸 Step 1: Find Height $h$

Height difference for a pendulum:

$$
h = L(1 - \cos\theta)
$$

Substitute:

$$
h = 1.0 \cdot (1 - \cos 15^\circ)
$$

$$
\cos 15^\circ \approx 0.966
$$

$$
h = 1 - 0.966 = 0.034 \, \text{m}
$$

---

## 🔸 Step 2: Find Speed $v$

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute:

$$
v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 0.034}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{0.6664}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.82 \, \text{m/s}
$$

---

## 📌 Final Answer

- Speed at the bottom:

$$
v \approx 0.82 \, \text{m/s}
$$
