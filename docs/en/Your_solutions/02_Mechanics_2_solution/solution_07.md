# Block on Block Problem – Notes

## 🔹 Given

- Top block: $m_1 = 5 \, \text{kg}$ (tied to wall → does not move)  
- Bottom block: $m_2 = 10 \, \text{kg}$ (force applied)  
- Horizontal force: $F = 45 \, \text{N}$  
- Coefficient of kinetic friction: $\mu_k = 0.2$  
- Gravity: $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$  

---

## 🧠 Step 1: Friction Force

The top block is tied → it **resists motion**.  
Friction between the blocks:

$$
f_{\text{top-bottom}} = \mu_k m_1 g
$$

$$
f_{\text{top-bottom}} = 0.2 \cdot 5 \cdot 9.8
$$

$$
f_{\text{top-bottom}} = 9.8 \, \text{N}
$$

Friction between bottom block and surface (if needed) is **not mentioned**, so assume smooth floor.  

---

## 🧠 Step 2: Net Force on Bottom Block

The **horizontal force applied** minus **friction from top block**:

$$
F_{\text{net}} = F - f_{\text{top-bottom}}
$$

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 45 - 9.8
$$

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 35.2 \, \text{N}
$$

---

## 🧠 Step 3: Acceleration of Bottom Block

Use Newton’s 2nd law:

$$
a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{m_2}
$$

$$
a = \frac{35.2}{10}
$$

$$
a \approx 3.52 \, \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

- Acceleration of the 10 kg block:

$$
a \approx 3.52 \, \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

## 📚 Key Concept

- Friction **opposes relative motion** between surfaces.  
- When a block is **tied to the wall**, its friction **resists motion** of the block below.  
- Use $f = \mu m g$ for kinetic friction.  
- Net force = applied force − friction → then $a = F_{\text{net}}/m$.
