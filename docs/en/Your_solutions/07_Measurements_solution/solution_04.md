# Speedometer Uncertainty Notes

## Given

A car’s speedometer has an uncertainty of:

$$
\pm 5\%
$$

The reading shown is:

$$
v = 60 \ \text{km/h}
$$

---

## Step 1: Convert percentage uncertainty to absolute uncertainty

We calculate 5% of 60 km/h:

$$
\Delta v = \frac{5}{100} \times 60
$$

$$
\Delta v = 3 \ \text{km/h}
$$

---

## Step 2: Determine actual speed range

The true speed can be lower or higher than the measured value:

$$
v_{\text{min}} = 60 - 3 = 57 \ \text{km/h}
$$

$$
v_{\text{max}} = 60 + 3 = 63 \ \text{km/h}
$$

---

## Final Answer

The actual speed of the car is in the range:

$$
57 \ \text{km/h} \leq v \leq 63 \ \text{km/h}
$$
