# Average Current of a Lightning Bolt

Given:

- Charge transferred:

$$
Q = 30\text{ C}
$$

- Time taken:

$$
t = 2\text{ ms}
$$

Convert milliseconds to seconds:

$$
2\text{ ms} = 2 \times 10^{-3}\text{ s}
$$

$$
t = 0.002\text{ s}
$$

---

# Formula for Current

Average current is calculated using:

$$
I = \frac{Q}{t}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
I = \frac{30}{0.002}
$$

$$
I = 15000\text{ A}
$$

---

# Final Answer

$$
\boxed{
I = 1.5 \times 10^4\text{ A}
}
$$

or

$$
\boxed{
I = 15000\text{ A}
}
$$

---

# Conclusion

The average current of the lightning bolt is:

$$
15000\text{ amperes}
$$

This shows that lightning carries an extremely large electric current in a very short amount of time.
