# Current from a Charge Function

Given the charge as a function of time:

$$
Q(t)=5t^2+5
$$

We need to find the current at:

$$
t=3\text{ s}
$$

---

# Relationship Between Charge and Current

Current is the rate of change of charge with respect to time:

$$
I(t)=\frac{dQ}{dt}
$$

Differentiate the charge function:

$$
Q(t)=5t^2+5
$$

Taking the derivative:

$$
I(t)=\frac{d}{dt}(5t^2+5)
$$

$$
I(t)=10t
$$

---

# Find the Current at $t=3\text{ s}$

Substitute:

$$
t=3
$$

into the current equation:

$$
I(3)=10(3)
$$

$$
I(3)=30\text{ A}
$$

---

# Final Answer

$$
\boxed{
I(3)=30\text{ A}
}
$$

---

# Conclusion

The current flowing through the wire at $t=3\text{ s}$ is:

$$
30\text{ A}
$$
