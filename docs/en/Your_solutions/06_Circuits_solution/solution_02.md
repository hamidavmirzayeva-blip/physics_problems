# Possible Equivalent Resistances Using Three $1\ \Omega$ Resistors

Given:

- Three identical resistors
- Each resistor has resistance:

$$
R = 1\ \Omega
$$

We find all unique equivalent resistances that can be formed using **one, two, or all three** resistors in series and/or parallel combinations.

---

# 1. Using One Resistor

$$
R_{eq} = 1\ \Omega
$$

---

# 2. Using Two Resistors

## a) Series Connection

For series:

$$
R_{eq} = R_1 + R_2
$$

$$
R_{eq} = 1 + 1
$$

$$
R_{eq} = 2\ \Omega
$$

---

## b) Parallel Connection

For parallel:

$$
\frac{1}{R_{eq}} =
\frac{1}{1} + \frac{1}{1}
$$

$$
\frac{1}{R_{eq}} = 2
$$

$$
R_{eq} = \frac{1}{2}\ \Omega
$$

---

# 3. Using Three Resistors

## a) All in Series

$$
R_{eq} = 1 + 1 + 1
$$

$$
R_{eq} = 3\ \Omega
$$

---

## b) All in Parallel

$$
\frac{1}{R_{eq}} =
1 + 1 + 1
$$

$$
\frac{1}{R_{eq}} = 3
$$

$$
R_{eq} = \frac{1}{3}\ \Omega
$$

---

## c) Two in Parallel, Then in Series with One

First combine two resistors in parallel:

$$
R_p = \frac{1 \cdot 1}{1 + 1}
$$

$$
R_p = \frac{1}{2}\ \Omega
$$

Now add the third resistor in series:

$$
R_{eq} = 1 + \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
R_{eq} = \frac{3}{2}\ \Omega
$$

---

## d) Two in Series, Then in Parallel with One

First combine two resistors in series:

$$
R_s = 1 + 1 = 2\ \Omega
$$

Now place this combination in parallel with the third resistor:

$$
\frac{1}{R_{eq}} =
\frac{1}{2} + 1
$$

$$
\frac{1}{R_{eq}} =
\frac{3}{2}
$$

$$
R_{eq} =
\frac{2}{3}\ \Omega
$$

---

# All Unique Equivalent Resistances

$$
\boxed{
\frac{1}{3},
\frac{1}{2},
\frac{2}{3},
1,
\frac{3}{2},
2,
3
\ \Omega
}
$$
