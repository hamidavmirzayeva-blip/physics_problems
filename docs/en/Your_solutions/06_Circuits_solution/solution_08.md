Capacitors in Parallel

In this problem, the current in the AC circuit is given by:

I(t)=2sin(120πt)

The circuit contains a single resistor with resistance:

R=50 Ω

To find the voltage across the resistor, we use Ohm’s Law for AC circuits:

V(t)=I(t)R

Substituting the given values:

V(t)=(2sin(120πt))(50)

Multiplying the constants:

V(t)=100sin(120πt)

Therefore, the voltage equation across the resistor is:

V(t)=100sin(120πt) V
