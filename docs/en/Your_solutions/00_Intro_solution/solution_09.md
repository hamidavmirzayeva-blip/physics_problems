* Curve: y = 3 − x²
* Rectangle is in the **first quadrant** (x ≥ 0, y ≥ 0)
* Rectangle **touches the curve** (so its top-right corner is on the curve)
* Find the rectangle with **maximum area**

---

### **Step 1: Express the area of the rectangle**

Let’s say:

* The **bottom-left corner** of the rectangle is at the origin (0,0)
* The **top-right corner** is at (x, y) on the curve

Then:

* Width of rectangle = x
* Height of rectangle = y = 3 − x²

So the **area** A(x) = width × height:

**A(x) = x * y = x * (3 − x²) = 3x − x³**

---

### **Step 2: Maximize the area**

To find maximum area, we use **derivatives**:

1. Take derivative of A(x):

**A'(x) = d/dx [3x − x³] = 3 − 3x²**

2. Set derivative = 0 to find critical points:

**3 − 3x² = 0**

Divide by 3:

**1 − x² = 0**

So:

**x² = 1 → x = 1** (we ignore x = −1 because we are in the first quadrant)

---

### **Step 3: Find the corresponding height**

The height is y = 3 − x²:

**y = 3 − 1² = 3 − 1 = 2**

---

### **Step 4: Verify maximum**

Second derivative:

**A''(x) = d/dx [3 − 3x²] = −6x**

At x = 1:

**A''(1) = −6 < 0** → confirms **maximum**

---

### ✅ **Step 5: Answer**

* **Width:** x = 1

* **Height:** y = 2

* **Maximum area:** A = 1 * 2 = 2
