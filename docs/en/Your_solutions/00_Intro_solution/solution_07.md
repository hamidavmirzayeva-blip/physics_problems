### Given:

* Bicycle is 10 meters from the wall.
* Bicycle moves toward the wall at 1 m/s.
* Fly starts at the bicycle and flies toward the wall at 2 m/s.
* When the fly hits the wall, it instantly turns back toward the bicycle.
* This continues until the bicycle hits the wall.

We want: total distance the fly travels.

---

### Step 1: Time until bicycle hits the wall

Time = distance / speed = 10 meters / 1 m/s = 10 seconds.

So the fly has **10 seconds** to fly before being crushed.

---

### Step 2: Fly’s total distance

The fly moves at 2 m/s, and flies the whole 10 seconds:

Distance = speed × time = 2 × 10 = 20 meters.

---

### Step 3: Explanation

Even though the fly goes back and forth many times:

* Its speed is constant, and
* It flies for exactly the time the bicycle reaches the wall.

So we **don’t need to calculate each back-and-forth trip**.

---

### ✅ Answer:

**20 meters**
