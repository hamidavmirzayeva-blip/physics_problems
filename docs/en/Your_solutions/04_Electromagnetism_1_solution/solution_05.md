# ⚡ Electric Field Required to Levitate a Proton

## 📌 Problem
Find the electric field strength needed to make a proton levitate against gravity.

Given:
mₚ = 1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ kg  
e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C  
g = 9.8 m/s²  

---

## 🧠 Key Idea

For levitation:
Electric force = Gravitational force  

Fe = Fg  

qE = mg  

So:
E = mg / q  

---

## 🔢 Calculation

Substitute values:

E = (1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ × 9.8) / (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)

Step-by-step:

m × g ≈ 1.64 × 10⁻²⁶  

E = (1.64 × 10⁻²⁶) / (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)

E ≈ 1.03 × 10⁻⁷ N/C  

---

## ✅ Final Answer

Required electric field:

E ≈ 1.0 × 10⁻⁷ N/C  

---

## 🧭 Direction

- Proton is positively charged  
- Electric force must act **upward** to balance gravity  

So, the electric field must point **upward**

---

## 🧠 Conclusion

A very small electric field is enough to balance gravity on a proton due to its extremely small mass.
