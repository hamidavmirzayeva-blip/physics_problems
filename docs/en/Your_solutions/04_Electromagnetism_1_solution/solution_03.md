# ⚖️ Equilibrium Position Between Two Charges

## 📌 Problem
Two charges:
q₁ = +4 C and q₂ = +9 C  
Distance between them = 2 m  

Find the position where a charge q₃ = +1 C is in equilibrium (net force = 0).

---

## 🧠 Key Idea

For equilibrium:
Net force on q₃ = 0  

Since all charges are positive, forces are **repulsive**, so equilibrium must lie **between the charges**.

---

## 📐 Step 1: Define Position

Let x = distance from q₁ to q₃  

Then distance from q₂:
(2 - x)

---

## ⚡ Step 2: Use Coulomb’s Law

For equilibrium:
Force from q₁ = Force from q₂

k * q₁ * q₃ / x² = k * q₂ * q₃ / (2 - x)²

Cancel k and q₃:

q₁ / x² = q₂ / (2 - x)²

---

## 🔢 Step 3: Substitute Values

4 / x² = 9 / (2 - x)²

Take square root:

2 / x = 3 / (2 - x)

---

## 🧮 Step 4: Solve

Cross multiply:

2(2 - x) = 3x

4 - 2x = 3x

4 = 5x

x = 0.8 m

---

## ✅ Final Answer

The equilibrium position is:

x = 0.8 m from q₁  

(or 1.2 m from q₂)

---

## 🧠 Conclusion

The point is closer to the **smaller charge (q₁ = 4 C)** because the larger charge (9 C) repels more strongly.
