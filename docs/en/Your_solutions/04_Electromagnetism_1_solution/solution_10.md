# 🧲 Magnetic Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

## 📌 Given
- Length of wire: L = 2.0 m  
- Current: I = 10 A  
- Magnetic field: B = 0.5 T  

Formula:
F = BIL sinθ  

---

# ⚡ Case (a): θ = 90°

sin 90° = 1  

F = 0.5 × 10 × 2 × 1  

F = 10 N  

---

# ⚡ Case (b): θ = 45°

sin 45° = √2 / 2 ≈ 0.707  

F = 0.5 × 10 × 2 × 0.707  

F = 10 × 0.707  

F ≈ 7.07 N  

---

# ⚡ Case (c): θ = 0°

sin 0° = 0  

F = 0.5 × 10 × 2 × 0  

F = 0 N  

---

# ✅ Final Answers

- (a) F = 10 N  
- (b) F ≈ 7.07 N  
- (c) F = 0 N  

---

# 🧠 Conclusion
- Force is maximum when wire is perpendicular to magnetic field  
- Force decreases with angle  
- No force when wire is parallel to field (θ = 0°)
