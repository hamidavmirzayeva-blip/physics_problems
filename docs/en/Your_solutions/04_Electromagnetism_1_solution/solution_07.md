# ⚡ Electron in Electric + Magnetic Field

## 📌 Given
- Potential difference: V = 5000 V  
- Magnetic field: B = 0.1 T  
- Electron starts from rest  
- Charge of electron: e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C  
- Mass of electron: m = 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg  

---

# ⚡ Step 1: Speed after acceleration

Energy gained from electric field becomes kinetic energy:

qV = (1/2)mv²

Solve for v:

v = √(2qV / m)

Substitute:

v = √(2 × 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ × 5000 / 9.11 × 10⁻³¹)

---

### Calculation:

2 × 1.6 × 5000 = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁵

v = √(1.6 × 10⁻¹⁵ / 9.11 × 10⁻³¹)

v = √(1.76 × 10¹⁵)

v ≈ 4.2 × 10⁷ m/s

---

# 🧲 Step 2: Magnetic force provides centripetal force

qvB = mv² / r

Solve for radius:

r = mv / (qB)

---

# 📐 Step 3: Substitute values

r = (9.11 × 10⁻³¹ × 4.2 × 10⁷) / (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ × 0.1)

---

### Numerator:
9.11 × 4.2 ≈ 38.3  
→ 3.83 × 10⁻²³

### Denominator:
1.6 × 10⁻²⁰

---

r = (3.83 × 10⁻²³) / (1.6 × 10⁻²⁰)

r ≈ 2.39 × 10⁻³ m

---

# ✅ Final Answer

Radius of circular path:

r ≈ 2.4 × 10⁻³ m  
= 2.4 mm

---

# 🧠 Conclusion
- Electron is accelerated by electric field → gains speed  
- Magnetic field bends its path into a circle  
- Higher speed → larger radius  
- Strong magnetic field → tighter curvature
