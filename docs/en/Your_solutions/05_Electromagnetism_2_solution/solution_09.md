# Refraction at an Air–Glass Boundary

Given:

- Refractive index of air:

$$
n_1 = 1.00
$$

- Refractive index of glass:

$$
n_2 = 1.50
$$

- Angle of incidence:

$$
\theta_1 = 30^\circ
$$

---

# Snell’s Law

The relationship between angles and refractive indices is given by:

$$
n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2
$$

We need to find $\theta_2$ (angle of refraction).

---

# Step 1: Substitute values

$$
1.00 \cdot \sin(30^\circ) = 1.50 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)
$$

Since:

$$
\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5
$$

We get:

$$
0.5 = 1.50 \sin(\theta_2)
$$

---

# Step 2: Solve for $\sin(\theta_2)$

$$
\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.50}
$$

$$
\sin(\theta_2) = 0.333
$$

---

# Step 3: Find angle

$$
\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}(0.333)
$$

$$
\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ
$$

---

# Final Answer

$$
\boxed{
\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ
}
$$

---

# Conclusion

The light bends toward the normal when entering glass because glass has a higher refractive index than air.
