# Orbital Speed Comparison: ISS vs Earth around the Sun

---

# 1. ISS Orbital Speed

## Given

Earth’s mass:

$$
M_E = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\ \text{kg}
$$

Gravitational constant:

$$
G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\ \text{N m}^2/\text{kg}^2
$$

Earth radius:

$$
R_E \approx 6378\ \text{km}
$$

ISS altitude:

$$
h = 400\ \text{km}
$$

Orbital radius:

$$
r = R_E + h = 6778\ \text{km} = 6.778 \times 10^6\ \text{m}
$$

---

## Step 1: Orbital Speed Formula

For circular orbit:

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{GM_E}{r}}
$$

---

## Step 2: Substitute Values

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})}{6.778 \times 10^6}}
$$

---

## Step 3: Compute

$$
v \approx \sqrt{5.87 \times 10^7}
$$

$$
v \approx 7660\ \text{m/s}
$$

Convert to km/s:

$$
v_{ISS} \approx 7.66\ \text{km/s}
$$

---

# 2. Earth Orbital Speed Around the Sun

## Given

Orbital radius:

$$
r = 150 \times 10^6\ \text{km}
$$

Orbital period:

$$
T = 365.25\ \text{days}
$$

Convert time:

$$
T = 365.25 \times 24 \times 3600 = 3.156 \times 10^7\ \text{s}
$$

---

## Step 1: Circumference of Orbit

$$
C = 2\pi r
$$

$$
C = 2\pi (150 \times 10^6)
$$

$$
C \approx 9.42 \times 10^8\ \text{km}
$$

---

## Step 2: Orbital Speed

$$
v = \frac{C}{T}
$$

$$
v = \frac{9.42 \times 10^8}{3.156 \times 10^7}
$$

$$
v \approx 29.9\ \text{km/s}
$$

---

# 3. Comparison

| Object | Speed |
|--------|------|
| ISS around Earth | **7.66 km/s** |
| Earth around Sun | **29.9 km/s** |

---

# Final Conclusion

$$
\boxed{\text{Earth’s orbital speed around the Sun is faster than the ISS orbital speed}}
$$

- ISS: ~7.7 km/s  
- Earth around Sun: ~30 km/s  

👉 Earth moves about **4 times faster** in its orbit around the Sun than the ISS moves around Earth.
