# Escape Velocity from the Moon

---

## 1. Given Data

Moon’s mass:

$$
M_M = 7.35 \times 10^{22}\ \text{kg}
$$

Moon’s radius:

$$
R_M = 1737\ \text{km} = 1.737 \times 10^6\ \text{m}
$$

Gravitational constant:

$$
G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\ \text{N m}^2/\text{kg}^2
$$

---

## 2. Escape Velocity Formula

Escape velocity is:

$$
v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}
$$

---

## 3. Substitute Values

$$
v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(7.35 \times 10^{22})}{1.737 \times 10^6}}
$$

---

## 4. Compute Step by Step

First compute numerator:

$$
2GM = 2 \times 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 7.35 \times 10^{22}
$$

$$
2GM \approx 9.80 \times 10^{12}
$$

Now divide by radius:

$$
\frac{2GM}{R} = \frac{9.80 \times 10^{12}}{1.737 \times 10^6}
$$

$$
\approx 5.64 \times 10^6
$$

Now square root:

$$
v_e \approx \sqrt{5.64 \times 10^6}
$$

$$
v_e \approx 2375\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## 5. Final Answer (km/s)

$$
v_e \approx 2.38\ \text{km/s}
$$

---

## 6. Comparison with Earth

Earth escape velocity:

$$
v_{E} = 11.2\ \text{km/s}
$$

Fraction:

$$
\frac{v_M}{v_E} = \frac{2.38}{11.2}
$$

$$
\approx 0.21
$$

---

## 7. Final Results

### Escape velocity from the Moon:

$$
\boxed{v_e \approx 2.38\ \text{km/s}}
$$

### As a fraction of Earth’s escape velocity:

$$
\boxed{\approx 0.21}
$$

---

## 8. Interpretation

- The Moon’s escape velocity is about **5 times smaller than Earth’s**
- This is because the Moon has:
  - Much smaller mass
  - Much smaller gravitational field

So leaving the Moon requires far less energy than leaving Earth.
