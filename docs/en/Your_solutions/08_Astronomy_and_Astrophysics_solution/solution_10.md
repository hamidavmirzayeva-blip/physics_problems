# Measuring the Speed of Light Using a Microwave Oven

## Given

Frequency of the microwave oven:

$$
f = 2.45 \times 10^9 \ \text{Hz}
$$

Accepted value of the speed of light:

$$
c_{\text{accepted}} = 3.00 \times 10^8 \ \text{m/s}
$$

Measured distance between adjacent melted spots:

$$
d = 6.1 \ \text{cm} = 0.061 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Step 1: Determine the Wavelength

The distance between two adjacent melted spots corresponds to half a wavelength:

$$
d = \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = 2d
$$

Substituting the measured value:

$$
\lambda = 2(0.061)
$$

$$
\lambda = 0.122 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Step 2: Calculate the Speed of Light

The speed of a wave is given by:

$$
c = f\lambda
$$

Substituting the values:

$$
c = (2.45 \times 10^9)(0.122)
$$

$$
c = 2.989 \times 10^8 \ \text{m/s}
$$

or

$$
c = 298,900,000 \ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## Step 3: Compare with the Accepted Value

Accepted value:

$$
c_{\text{accepted}} = 300,000,000 \ \text{m/s}
$$

Measured value:

$$
c_{\text{measured}} = 298,900,000 \ \text{m/s}
$$

Difference:

$$
\Delta c = |c_{\text{accepted}} - c_{\text{measured}}|
$$

$$
\Delta c = |300,000,000 - 298,900,000|
$$

$$
\Delta c = 1,100,000 \ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## Step 4: Calculate the Percentage Error

$$
\text{Percentage Error} =
\frac{|300000000 - 298900000|}
{300000000}
\times 100
$$

Therefore, the percentage error is **0.37%**.
