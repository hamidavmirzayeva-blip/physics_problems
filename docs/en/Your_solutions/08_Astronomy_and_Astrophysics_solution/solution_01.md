# Linear Speed at the Earth's Equator

## Given

Earth's radius:

$$
R = 6378 \ \text{km}
$$

Time for one complete rotation:

$$
T = 24 \ \text{h}
$$

Convert time to seconds:

$$
T = 24 \times 3600 = 86400 \ \text{s}
$$

---

## Step 1: Calculate the Circumference of the Earth

The distance traveled by a point on the equator in one rotation is the Earth's circumference:

$$
C = 2\pi R
$$

Substituting the value of the radius:

$$
C = 2\pi(6378)
$$

$$
C \approx 40074 \ \text{km}
$$

---

## Step 2: Calculate the Linear Speed

Linear speed is distance divided by time:

$$
v = \frac{C}{T}
$$

Substituting the values:

$$
v = \frac{40074}{86400}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.464 \ \text{km/s}
$$

---

## Final Answer

The linear speed of a point on the Earth's equator due to Earth's rotation is:

$$
\boxed{v \approx 0.464 \ \text{km/s}}
$$
